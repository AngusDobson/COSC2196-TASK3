SUMMARY

During the transition from Assignment 2 to 3, the Gravy Seals team continued to showcase how well they are able to adapt and collaborate with each other, regardless of geographical location and time zone disparities. Our team continued to regularly meet every Tuesday and Saturday, with those unable to meet watching over meeting recordings and making any comments or contributions that they saw fit.

Aside from the regular meetings, our team would continue to check in with each other daily on MS Teams to see how each task is progressing and if anyone was stuck or needed any help on their sections. Lessons learnt from working with the Gravy Seals team is how invaluable regular check ins are with each other as they gave us an insight to how the project was progressing.

The collaboration between the group was again very productive, with all team members noting equal contributions amongst the entire team not only in completing designated tasks, but also in taking initiative, putting their hand up to contribute more and driving the projects progression forward.

While the team was productive there were still some challenges along the way. Synchronizing team meetings and balancing individual team members personal commitments and priorities was again at times difficult, although the foundations set from assignment 2 reinforced the teams trust in each other. Each individual was personally responsible for their own work and ensured that they met their objectives and met the deadlines and expectations from the team.

On the other hand, a highlight was the teams early initiative to setup the website template, which allowed for simultaneous contributions instead of a last minute rush to upload all the content in the final week. Our GitHub log also reflects contributions relatively well as we asked for each team member to be responsible for uploading their own work so that all members would have some contributions logged. In the final week of the assignment leading up to the due date, our team was able to come together and make quick decisions on any last minute additions or work that we can do to the project that might add value to the project overall. This was when the decision to build a working demo of our project was made.
