PROJECT DESCRIPTION

OVERVIEW

Topic

The proposed project revolves around the development of Dinnerizer, a digital meal planning tool designed for mobile and desktop platforms. This application aims to streamline the meal preparation process, offering a multitude of dinner suggestions tailored to users' dietary needs, preferences, and ingredient availability. With an organized grocery list generated for each meal plan, Dinnerizer aims to facilitate efficient shopping, reduce food waste, and promote diverse home-cooked meals. The motivation behind this project lies in enhancing the overall cooking experience, making meal planning more efficient, enjoyable, and accessible for all users, irrespective of their dietary preferences or physical abilities.

Motivation

The motivation for the Dinnerizer project stems from the need to simplify daily meal decisions, which are often time-consuming and stressful. This project aligns with the growing IT trends in the field of food technology and mobile applications, with an emphasis on personalization and accessibility. It will demonstrate our ability to apply technical skills in real-world contexts, creating a practical, user-friendly solution to an everyday problem. Completing this project will demonstrate our team's proficiency in Java programming, UI/UX design, data management, and responsiveness to user feedback. Future employers will value this experience, seeing our capabilities in application development, problem-solving, and understanding user needs.

Landscape

The current landscape of meal planning applications is relatively diverse, with apps such as Mealime (Mealime, 2023), Plan to Eat (Plan to Eat, 2023), and Yummly (Yummly, 2023) offering various features ranging from personalized meal plans to dietary tracking. However, Dinnerizer distinguishes itself with its focus on reducing food waste and promoting accessibility. This focus aligns with the Food and Agriculture Organization's emphasis on food waste reduction and the role of technology (Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, 2021). The app recommends meals based on the specific ingredients that users already have, which is a feature not common in many existing apps. Dinnerizer fills a gap in the market for a more inclusive meal planning app by considering users with disabilities. Its commitment to continuous improvement via user feedback, combined with the use of lightweight databases like SQLite for improved scalability and search functionality (SQLite, 2023), add a layer of innovation to this project, demonstrating the application of machine learning principles to the profession of meal planning (Darcy, Louie, and Roberts, 2016).

DETAILED DESCRIPTION

Aims – Revolutionise meal-planning

The aim of Dinnerizer is to revolutionize meal planning by creating an innovative meal planning app that provides comprehensive meal-plans for users, this will streamline how they plan for meals, as well as aid in grocery shopping. By making a user-friendly and personalized app. Dinnerizer seeks to redefine how people plan their meals, making it more efficient, enjoyable, and timesaving.

Goal 1:

If this project is to succeed, we need to create an extensive database of dinner options that are tailored to users’ preferences, diet, and lifestyle. This goal is necessary as a strong database will enable the app to generate personalized meal plans that can cater to their individual tastes, and consider all factors like dietary restrictions, allergies, cultural preferences etc. If we can achieve this goal, it will lay the foundation for a highly customizable meal-planning experience.

Goal 2:

The second goal is to create an intelligent recommendation system using data science. If we analyze user behavior and meal choices, the application will offer smart, data-driven meal suggestions that can adapt and improve over time. This goal is particularly vital as it will enhance user engagement, increase product satisfaction, as well as promote healthier and more diverse eating habits among users.

Goal 3:

The Third goal is to integrate a comprehensive grocery shopping feature for the application. This goal will complement the meal planning process and ensure that users can manage their shopping lists based on the meals they have selected. This system should minimize waste and identify items that the user is commonly purchasing.

Goal 4:

The last goal is to ensure a seamless user experience across as many devices and platforms as possible. Developing a responsive, user-friendly interface for both mobile and web versions is especially critical in achieving widespread usage. If we can prioritize a consistent and intuitive user interface, the application will appeal to a broader audience and encourage long-term usage.

With these four goals outlined we can guide the project's development and decision-making process. If challenges or constraints are to arise, these goals can assist us in prioritizing the most important aspects of the project and ensure that the core features related to revolutionizing meal planning are given the highest priority.

PLANS AND PROGRESS

Story:

The idea for the Dinnerizer app was born out of a shared frustration among the team members with the tediousness of meal planning and grocery shopping. We saw an opportunity to revolutionize the way people plan their meals and make it more efficient, personalized, and enjoyable. Our vision was to create a comprehensive meal planning application that would cater to a user's individual preferences, dietary needs, and lifestyle choices while also attempting to automate the entire process of creating a grocery shopping list.

Progress:

Over the past weeks, we have made considerable progress towards developing the Dinnerizer application. We began by doing market research by analyzing comparable products on the market to understand what exactly is available, which includes what their products are bringing to the table, as well as the key points where their products are lacking which has helped us to pinpoint key aspects to focus on.

Technical Development:

The technical development for Dinnerizer will begin with creating a scalable backend infrastructure. We will have our Lead Software Developer lead the team in designing and implementing a backend system. With this we can have a solid foundation for handling user data, authentication and managing dinner options.

User Interface and Experience:

Our UI/UX designer role will be working hard on wireframes and mock-ups to design a visually appealing and intuitive user interface. Once these are completed, we will be able to conduct usability testing and have a look at the feedback, which will enable us to make informed design alterations. As a result of that we can create a truly user-friendly interface that will guide users through the meal planning process seamlessly.

Grocery Shopping Integration:

To enhance the grocery shopping experience, we are working on integrating the application with popular online grocery platforms and creating an algorithm to optimize users’ shopping lists. By analyzing the meal plans, the system will be able to generate an optimized shopping list, which will reduce waste and ensure a smooth grocery shopping process.

Current Stage:

As of now, we are at the infant stages of development, but when these core features are implemented and tested we can focus on refining the user experience, ensuring cross-platform compatibility, and conducting extensive testing to identify and address any potential issues before our official launch.

Future Plans:

Moving forward, our plans involve launching the Dinnerizer application on both iOS and Android platforms, as well as making it accessible as a Web Application. We want to collaborate with a range of people including professional chefs and nutritionists to expand the database of options and provide users with a wider variety of recipes to align with their goals and keep the quality of our product high.

Handover:

If we were to hand this project over to a new team, we would need ensure that our engineers are keeping detailed documentation on the application architecture, data models, APIS etc so that they are able to pick up where we left off. We would also share our research findings and feedback on testing so that the new team can understand the needs of users and capitalize on this information. We would also need our code repository to be organized so that we can have a smooth transition on code handover.

Conclusion:

The Dinnerizer project has come a long way since its initial inception. We are so excited about the affect this application could have meal planning for both individuals and families! With our aim of revolutionizing meal planning, we have the opportunity to develop a feature-rich app that will cater to all users’ needs and preferences. We believe that Dinnerizer has the potential to become the market leader as a meal planning platform, which will simplify the lives of users and encourage healthier eating habits! As we move to the development stage, we are confident that we have laid the groundwork for future growth of the app.

  • Lead Software Developer

The Product Manager role ensures the overall success of the project, by leading feature design, direction and priority. The Product Manager role will spend time understanding the market trends and needs of our customers. The combination of project management and customer specification elicitation skills enables the Dinnerizer project to deliver a project that meets the customers' needs, and aligns with the business goals and priorities.

The Lead Software Developer will guide the technical direction of the project, establishing and maintaining software development standards through technical expertise and leadership. Together with the Product Manager, the Lead Software Developer will create a project roadmap to inform technical specifications that the development team will follow.

The Software Developer role is responsible for delivering the Product Manager and Lead Software Developers project to fruition by translating the technical specifications and project roadmap into features. As individual contributors, they will focus on building both frontend and backend components out for the Dinnerizer project.

The UI/UX Designers work with the Product Manager and Software Developers to design intuitive and visually appealing interfaces that strengthen satisfaction and engagement from the user. The UI/UX Designers enable the Product Manager to visualize their product features aiding with feedback and iterative feature design, working to finalize designs prior to the development of the frontend components.

SCOPE AND LIMITS

During the development phase of our Dinnerizer project, the project team will be focused on delivering the features that have been defined by the Product Manager, and any supporting features for the technical platform as set out by the Lead Software Developer. The Product Manager and Lead Software Developer will work together to define a clear scope and roadmap, ensuring that we’re only delivering what’s minimally viable for the project.

The project will focus on providing users the ability to receive recipe suggestions based on their dietary needs and availability of ingredients. The project will not include the features related to incorporating a users preference for meals by cuisine or taste. Additionally, the project will focus on generating organised shopping lists for the recipes that have been suggested, and will remove features related to reducing food waste and efficiencies in shopping from the initial scope. This enables the team to focus on the core functionality of the application and will enable the Product Manager to gather feedback and usage insights from users. This feedback will guide the future roadmap of the project if it is decided to be worth pursuing.

TESTING

This project will run through six different testing stages throughout its development to ensure its functionality, usability, and performance. We will continuously test the project as we are developing it.

1. Functional Testing

Functional Testing will be carried out to ensure that the Dinnerizer application works as intended. During this stage, we will test different use cases and scenarios to identify and fix any bugs or glitches.

2. Usability Testing

Usability testing will be carried out to test the ease of use of the application to make sure that it is user friendly and intuitive. This stage will evaluate the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) of Dinnerizer. This test will be performed by both technical and non-technical users.

3. Performance Testing

Performance Testing will be carried out to assess how the application runs under different conditions such as device, network speeds, user requests and will measure response and loading times.

4. Security Testing

Security Testing will be undertaken to identify vulnerabilities in the application and ensure that personal user data is protected. We will use an external company to test the security of the application.

5. User Acceptance Testing (UAT)

We will engage real, non-technical users from different backgrounds to test our application. We will invite them to interact with the app and provide feedback on its features and any bugs that they discover.

6. Beta Testing

Before the initial release, we will release a beta version of the application online to friends and family and collect feedback from them to uncover any bugs or issues before the full release of the app to the public.

Our technical test users will be our team members, who have full knowledge of the application and how it is intended to run. We will find our non-technical test users through our friends, families, and online communities. For diversity of different technical skillsets, we will aim to invite around 30 test users ranging from ages 15 to 60 years old. The diversity in background and age will help us to identify a wider range of issues.

RISKS

In general, all projects will come with risks no matter how big or small a business is. A startup group like us comes with significantly more risk than an established business. We may have to purchase tools and technology to be able to complete our project which will come with the risk of the tech we purchase not being able to fully complete the task we need it to complete. This of course can be mitigated by detailed research into each type of tool and technology noting all its advantages and disadvantages.

Stemming from this risk and the most important thing to be careful about is financial risk. The team could certainly opt to only utilize the resources, tools and technologies they already own, which would essentially remove financial risk from the equation completely. However, if we want to compete in the market for paying customers, we may need to branch out and purchase additional tools and technologies or even pitch our application to potential investors.

If we were to succeed in acquiring investors, that would then add another layer of risk. As investors will want a return on their investment and may even want a deadline for a working application to be completed by. If the Dinnerizer fails to produce a return, then the group members may have to pay investors back with our own money or the investors could pull out of the project completely.

GROUP PROCESSES AND COMMUNICATIONS

From the onset of forming the gravy seals, the group has been meeting twice a week on teams using the inbuilt meeting function. As our members are located all over the world with different time zones, the meetings have been designed to cater as best we can for all members. However, not all members can attend every meeting and for this reason we record the meetings and send out notes afterwards. If a member misses the meeting, they are required to watch the recording and add any comments to the general discussion in teams.

If a group member does not respond to communications, we give roughly a one-week leniency because most members have jobs and live in different time zones. If they fail to communicate within the week one member will reach out to that member directly for any clarifications. If no response from a direct message, then the team would have no choice but to escalate it to the RMIT staff. Fortunately, this has not happened with any member of the gravy seals.
